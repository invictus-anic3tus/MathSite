Arithmetic

Arithmetic: The branch of mathematics dealing with numbers and basic operations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Integer: A whole number that can be positive, negative, or zero, but not a fraction or decimal.

Prime Number: A number greater than 1 that has no divisors other than 1 and itself.

Factorial: The product of all positive integers less than or equal to a given number (denoted as n!). For example, 5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120.

Geometry

Geometry: The branch of mathematics concerned with the properties and relations of points, lines, surfaces, and solids.

Angle: The figure formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint, measured in degrees or radians.

Triangle: A polygon with three sides and three angles. Common types include equilateral, isosceles, and scalene triangles.

Circle: A set of all points in a plane that are a fixed distance (radius) from a given point (center).

Algebra

Algebra: The branch of mathematics dealing with symbols and rules for manipulating those symbols, representing numbers and relationships.

Variable: A symbol (often a letter) used to represent an unknown value in an equation or expression.

Equation: A statement that two expressions are equal, often containing one or more variables to solve for.

Polynomial: An algebraic expression consisting of terms that are constants or variables raised to whole-number powers.

Linear Algebra

Matrix: A rectangular array of numbers arranged in rows and columns, used in linear algebra to solve systems of equations or transformations.

Vector: A quantity with both magnitude and direction, represented by an arrow in geometry or as an array of numbers in algebra.

Determinant: A scalar value that can be computed from the elements of a square matrix, used to determine whether a matrix is invertible.

Eigenvalue: A scalar that indicates how much a corresponding eigenvector is scaled during a linear transformation.

Calculus

Calculus: A branch of mathematics focusing on limits, functions, derivatives, integrals, and infinite series.

Derivative: A measure of how a function changes as its input changes, representing the slope of the function at any given point.

Integral: A fundamental concept in calculus representing the area under a curve or the accumulation of quantities.

Limit: The value that a function or sequence approaches as the input or index approaches some value.
